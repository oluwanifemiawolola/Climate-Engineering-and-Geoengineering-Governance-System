# Climate Engineering and Geoengineering Governance System

A comprehensive blockchain-based governance framework for overseeing climate intervention technologies, ensuring responsible research, deployment, and international coordination of geoengineering activities.

## System Overview

This system consists of five interconnected smart contracts that provide transparent, decentralized governance for climate engineering activities:

### 1. Solar Radiation Management Oversight Contract
- **Purpose**: Governs research and deployment of solar radiation management (SRM) technologies
- **Key Features**:
    - Research proposal submission and approval
    - Deployment authorization and monitoring
    - Safety protocol enforcement
    - Stakeholder voting mechanisms

### 2. Carbon Dioxide Removal Verification Contract
- **Purpose**: Validates and tracks large-scale carbon dioxide removal (CDR) projects
- **Key Features**:
    - Project registration and verification
    - Carbon credit issuance and tracking
    - Performance monitoring and reporting
    - Quality assurance protocols

### 3. Weather Modification Regulation Contract
- **Purpose**: Oversees cloud seeding and other weather modification activities
- **Key Features**:
    - Activity licensing and permits
    - Geographic boundary enforcement
    - Impact assessment requirements
    - Cross-border coordination protocols

### 4. Climate Intervention Risk Assessment Contract
- **Purpose**: Evaluates potential unintended consequences of geoengineering
- **Key Features**:
    - Risk scoring and classification
    - Impact prediction modeling
    - Mitigation strategy development
    - Emergency response protocols

### 5. International Climate Governance Contract
- **Purpose**: Coordinates global climate intervention policies and agreements
- **Key Features**:
    - Multi-national treaty management
    - Dispute resolution mechanisms
    - Resource allocation coordination
    - Compliance monitoring and enforcement

## Technical Architecture

### Data Structures
- **Proposals**: Research and deployment proposals with voting mechanisms
- **Projects**: Active climate intervention projects with monitoring data
- **Assessments**: Risk evaluations and impact studies
- **Treaties**: International agreements and coordination protocols
- **Stakeholders**: Authorized participants with defined roles and permissions

### Governance Model
- **Multi-stakeholder approach**: Scientists, policymakers, affected communities
- **Consensus-based decision making**: Weighted voting based on expertise and stake
- **Transparency requirements**: All activities recorded on-chain
- **Accountability mechanisms**: Performance tracking and penalty systems

## Contract Interactions

### For Researchers
1. Submit research proposals through SRM Oversight Contract
2. Register CDR projects for verification
3. Request risk assessments for new interventions

### For Policymakers
1. Review and vote on proposals
2. Set regulatory parameters
3. Coordinate international agreements

### For Communities
1. Participate in stakeholder voting
2. Submit impact reports
3. Access transparency data

## Compliance and Standards

The system enforces:
- **Environmental standards**: Minimum safety and efficacy requirements
- **Ethical guidelines**: Informed consent and equitable impact distribution
- **Scientific rigor**: Peer review and evidence-based decision making
- **International law**: Compliance with existing climate treaties
